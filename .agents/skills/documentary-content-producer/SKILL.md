# Documentary Content Producer

Use this skill before writing public-facing Job Search OS content.

## Workflow

1. Treat the user's prompt as the assignment. Do not let a random recent plan or stale build-log item become the main angle.
2. Pick one concrete evidence anchor before drafting: plan source, agent run, analytics fact, screenshot, grounded claim, or documented decision.
3. Use a documentary structure: scene, evidence, decision, consequence, artifact, takeaway.
4. Match the format to the job:
   - `field_note`: observation from the work plus the artifact that proved it.
   - `lesson`: what changed after the evidence forced a different decision.
   - `teardown`: what was weak, what replaced it, and why.
   - `visual_walkthrough`: what the screen/chart now helps the user understand.
   - `product_thesis`: a claim backed by one artifact and one consequence.
5. Keep the voice candid and specific. Use short paragraphs, concrete nouns, and visible tradeoffs.
6. Keep public safety intact: aggregate facts only, no company names, job URLs, salaries, recruiters, emails, private outcomes, viewer identities, or unsupported traction.

## Quality Bar

- Every draft must contain a real evidence anchor.
- The first third of the draft must make clear what artifact or system behavior is being discussed.
- The post must explain why the change matters to a reader who does not know the app.
- The final takeaway must be earned by the evidence, not pasted on as a slogan.

## Banned Patterns

- Do not open with "One plan in the build log keeps pulling me back."
- Do not reuse "documentarian loop" as filler.
- Do not say agents are "choosing from a static category" unless the source evidence is specifically about content generation.
- Do not write a generic progress update when the prompt asks about a screen, workflow, chart, architecture, or decision.
- Do not make the content sound like investor marketing, a launch announcement, or an abstract AI manifesto.
